Bio

I am a Computer Science PhD student, working under the guidance of Tejas Gokhale in the UMBC Cognitive Vision Group at University of Maryland, Baltimore County (UMBC). I work on interactive video retrieval/search, visual reasoning, and improving/assessing reliability for vision systems. My research in interactive video retrieval and search spans four key areas:

  1. Enhancing few-shot and zero-shot video search and retrieval by leveraging the rapid progress in Vision-Language Models (VLMs).
  2. Developing Scene Graph-based Chain-of-Thought reasoning frameworks to enable structured and interpretable understanding, retrieval, and search across complex video content.
  3. Investigating Video Question Answering (VQA) systems as auxiliary tasks for finetuning, with a focus on how the completeness of visual information affects downstream video understanding.
  4. Designing dialogue-driven interactive retrieval systems, where natural conversations guide iterative video exploration and search, improving user engagement and retrieval effectiveness.

I also focus on visual reasoning, with particular emphasis on:

  1. Understanding spatial relationships and transformations between objects, supporting more grounded and context-aware visual inference.
  2. Studying counterfactual visual reasoning, where hypothetical alterations to visual scenes help probe causality and explainability in model behavior.
  3. Exploring techniques for image and model editing, enabling controlled modifications and interpretability of vision systems through learned or rule-based interventions.
To ensure reliability in modern vision models, I pursue robust evaluation and mitigation strategies, including:
  1. Detecting and quantifying hallucinations in Vision-Language Models, identifying where and how models generate unsupported or fabricated content.
  2. Assessing the quality of generative models, with a particular focus on text-to-image (T2I) and text-to-video (T2V) outputs, measuring both fidelity and alignment to prompts.
Prior to joining UMBC, I was a Research Assistant at Center for Cognitive Skill Enhancement (CCSE) at Independent University Bangladesh (IUB). I have received a Masters of Science in Computer Science from UMBC in 2023 and a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from BRAC University in 2017. In my free time I love composing EDMs, cooking and driving. If not computer science, I would have pursued stand up comedy!
